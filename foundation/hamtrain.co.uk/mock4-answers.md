# MOCK TEST 4 — ANSWERS

## Question 1 — B

The three UK Amateur Radio licence levels are Foundation, Intermediate and Full.

## Question 2 — D

You need to make sure your station is identified when you change mode.

The wording of the licence says that the callsign must be "given in voice or other appropriate format consistent with the mode of operation."

Typically, your callsign will be embedded in the image that you're sending.

## Question 3 — C

If a station refuses to give a valid amateur radio callsign, this could be an unlicensed operator.

The amateur radio licence only allows you to communicate with other licence-holders, so in this case, you should terminate the contact.

## Question 4 — C

The licence issued by Ofcom requires that the Licensee must ensure that the station is not causing Undue Interference to other radio users.

## Question 5 — C

Refer to the "Foundation Licence Parameters" table in the 4 page exam booklet. Look up each value. The range 430 to 431MHz has a limit of 25W ERP (last column)

## Question 6 — A

The licence requires that you make available to Ofcom records to show that you comply with the requirements relating to electromagnetic field exposure. Keep a copy of your assessments.

## Question 7 — B

Potential Difference (better known as Voltage) is measured in Volts, Current in Amps and Resistance in Ohms.

## Question 8 — A

The range for normal human hearing is 20Hz to 15,000Hz. For some reason, this is a range you're required to remember for the Foundation exam.

## Question 9 — A

In a digital radio, a DAC is a Digital to Analogue Converter. This is a device used to represent a digital signal in an analogue format.

## Question 10 — C

Amplitude Modulation (AM), Frequency Modulation (FM), and Single Sideband (LSB and USB) are types of modulation. HF is not a type of modulation - it refers to the High Frequency part of the radio spectrum.

## Question 11 — B

The oscillator generates the radio frequency carrier, which defines the transmit frequency. This is then mixed with the audio. See the Transmitter block diagram in the training material for more information.

## Question 12 — A

Box 1 of the receiver block diagram handles tuning into the signal and amplification of the selected signal.

Box 2 is the demodulator

Box 3 is the audio amplifier

## Question 13 — D

Coaxial cable carries the RF signal on the centre conductor, which is surrounded by an outer screen.

## Question 14 — A

If there is a mismatch between the transmitter and the antenna system impedance, energy (in the form of standing waves) will be reflected back to the transmitter, potentially causing damage to the transmitter. An SWR meter is used to measure a mismatch.

## Question 15 — C

On a PL-259 plug, the screen of the coaxial feeder should be connected to the chassis (the outside metal part) of the connector.

## Question 16 — A

An "open" band supports Skywave propagation, meaning that signals can be bounced off the ionosphere, supporting contact on that frequency band.

## Question 17 — D

The most effective way to extend the range for transmitting and receiving VHF or UHF signals is to increase the height of the antenna. This extends the line of sight range.

## Question 18 — D

Interference can come into the TV through various routes, including the surround sound amplifier, speaker leads, and cabling, as well as from the antenna or mains supply.

## Question 19 — A

Electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) is the avoidance of interference between various pieces of electronic equipment.

## Question 20 — A

EMC (Electromagnetic compatibility) is all about the prevention of interference. There's a risk that your amateur radio station may cause interference to your neighbour's TV and radio, hence the possibility of disputes.

## Question 21 — C

If you hear something offensive on amateur radio, the advice is to ignore it and make no reference to it, as the offender may be looking for a reaction.

## Question 22 — D

Transmitting into a repeater will result in your signal being relayed over a wider area. Repeaters are primarily designed to allow mobile stations to make contact over greater distances.

## Question 23 — A

When giving signal reports, amateurs use "RST" - Readability, Signal Strength, and Tone. "Tone" is for Morse or Data. A strong signal with very good audio would be "59" (voice) or "599" (data)

## Question 24 — B

The answer required for the exam is that high voltages carry a risk of electrocution and high currents carry a risk of overheating and fire.

*Did you answer "electrocution?" Yes, high current flowing across the body can cause electrocution, but in terms of risk (and getting a mark in the exam), the answer is overheating.*

## Question 25 — C

For safety reasons, when working at height, you should wear a hard hat.

## Question 26 — B

The main health effect of exposure to electromagnetic radiation is heating of body tissue, and the eyes are particularly susceptible to damage.
