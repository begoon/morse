# MOCK TEST 5 — ANSWERS

## Question 1 — C

Amateur radio is non-commercial, so you are not allowed to advertise or sell your products over amateur radio or run a business using amateur radio.

Codes (such as Morse code) are allowed, as long as the code is not secret.

## Question 2 — C

The wording of the licence says that the callsign must be "given in voice or other appropriate format consistent with the mode of operation."

If you answered A or B, these are invalid, because you cannot transmit your callsign before you start transmitting.

## Question 3 — D

The amateur radio licence does not allow you to "Broadcast" (send a message to lots of amateurs or listeners). If you were talking to another amateur, you could mention the traffic problem to them, but you can't broadcast announcements.

## Question 4 — D

Ofcom has the power to require you to keep a log, make changes to reduce interference or restrict how you operate.

## Question 5 — A

Refer to the "Foundation Licence Parameters" table in the 4-page exam booklet. Look for the entry "3500-3800kHz" on page 2 (5 lines down). This is listed as "Primary. Shared with other services"

## Question 6 — D

You are required not to expose the public to EMF (electromagnetic fields) over agreed limits, and you must keep a record of assessments that prove you aren't exposing the public to EMF over those limits.

## Question 7 — B

milli (with a small 'm') is one-thousandth. Move the decimal place 3 times to the left, so 5mΩ (5 milliohms) is 0.005 Ohms.

## Question 8 — D

Wavelength is measured in meters. It's a lowercase letter "m" for "metres"

## Question 9 — A

ADC stands for Analogue to Digital Conversion. For more, see the digital signals section of Technical Basics.

## Question 10 — A

The oscillator handles the frequency generation, creating a "carrier" on the required radio frequency. The audio signal is then added to the carrier to make the radio signal.

## Question 12 — A

The detector (also known as the demodulator) stage recovers the original information from the modulated signal.

## Question 13 — C

Coaxial cable is screened, i.e. it has an outer screen that keeps the signal in and interference out.

## Question 14 — A

EIRP, Effective Isotropic Radiated Power, is a way of expressing antenna gain relative to a theoretical antenna that radiates equally in all directions.

Antenna manufacturers typically quote EIRP as it shows a better gain than ERP.

## Question 15 — D

If your antenna is not a perfect match, some of the power sent from the transmitter will be reflected back to the transmitter. These are known as Standing Waves, measured by an SWR meter.

## Question 16 — C

For the Foundation exam, remember that the Ionosphere comprises layers of conductive gases at heights between 70 and 400km above the Earth.

## Question 17 — D

It's the Troposphere. Conditions such as "Sporadic E" or "Tropospheric ducting" can allow VHF/UHF comms over wider-than-expected distances.

If you answered "Ionosphere", that's HF, not VHF/UHF.

## Question 18 — A

The ability of any piece of electronic or radio equipment to function correctly in the presence of strong RF signals is known as immunity.

## Question 19 — C

The function of a good RF earth is to provide a path to ground and minimise the chance of radio signals getting into the mains earth.

## Question 20 — C

## Question 21 — B

You should look for the 'centre of activity' for the mode in question, as this indicates where other stations using the same mode will be operating.

## Question 22 — B

Continuous Tone-Coded Squelch System is a tone transmitted with your audio signal. When you access a repeater using the correct tone, the repeater knows to "open" and relay your message.

## Question 23 — D

The microphone gain may need to be adjusted, and having another amateur give you a report on your audio signal helps you to check that your signal is not distorting.

## Question 24 — C

Lithium batteries, in particular, can cause fire and explosion if not properly treated.

## Question 25 — A

For safety reasons, when working at height, a tool belt or similar device to carry tools should be used, as it will help prevent falling objects.

## Question 26 — D

Touching a transmitting antenna, cable trip hazards and short-circuiting batteries all present risks at an amateur radio field event, so care is needed.
